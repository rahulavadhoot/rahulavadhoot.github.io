About Me

I'm a 26 year old Data Scientist with a background in Chemical Engineering. I'm currently based out of Los Angeles, California where I work at an AI startup for drug design. I enjoy machine learning, physics, chemistry and all things data science. My ultimate goal is to work on data science and AI applications that impact our world and tranform our society! Outside of work, I love long walks, classical music, landscape photography, abstract art and video games.

Data Science

My passion for data science started as a graduate student at the University of Washington. I was selected for the NSF sponsored Data-Intensive Research Enabling Clean Technologies (DIRECT) graduate training program - a data science program covering coursework and a capstone project at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL).

My MS Thesis work focused on developing PyMolSAR, an open-source Python package for computing molecular descriptors and predicting properties of small molecules.

As a Data Science Resident at Qulab Inc., I currently work on ADMET property prediction and QSAR modeling for drug lead optimization.
